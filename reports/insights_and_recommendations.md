## 3\. Department Performance

### Finding:

* **Dermatology** is the most visited department (210 appointments — 21% of total).
* **Cardiology** ranks second (151 appointments — 15.1%).
* **Gynecology** has the lowest footfall (50 appointments — 5%).

### Recommendation:

* Expand Dermatology capacity (hire 2–3 more dermatologists or add slots).
* Investigate why Gynecology has low visits — consider awareness campaigns.

\---

## 4\. Doctor Workload Analysis

### Finding:

* Top doctor (Dr. Doctor\_28, Cardiology) handled 20 completed appointments —
significantly above the average of \~11.5.
* Some doctors show 0 completed appointments (marked "Busy" or "On Leave").

### Recommendation:

* Redistribute appointments from overloaded doctors using automated scheduling.
* Put underutilized doctors on high-demand specialty rotations.

\---

## 5\. Appointment Trends

### Finding:

* Appointments peak in **June–August** (summer months).
* February is consistently the lowest month across all years.
* Year-over-year, appointment volume has grown \~15%.

### Recommendation:

* Pre-plan doctor availability during peak months (May–August).
* Introduce health camps or promotional consults in Feb to fill low-demand periods.

\---

## 6\. Treatment Cost Analysis

### Finding:

* **Arthritis** treatments are the most expensive (avg ₹8,955 per case).
* **Migraine** treatments are the least expensive (avg ₹6,992).
* Appendicitis and Hypertension together account for the most cases (144 total).

### Recommendation:

* Negotiate bulk medicine procurement for Arthritis and Asthma (high cost + volume).
* Create dedicated chronic-disease management packages for Hypertension \& Diabetes.

\---

## 7\. Cancellation Analysis

### Finding:

* Cancellation rate is **22.2%** — above the industry benchmark of 15%.
* No-Show rate is **10.1%**.

### Recommendation:

* Send SMS/WhatsApp reminders 24h and 2h before appointments.
* Implement a 4-hour cancellation policy with a nominal fee for late cancellations.
* Allow easy rescheduling via app to reduce outright cancellations.

\---

## 8\. Patient Demographics

### Finding:

* **Chennai** has the highest patient count (11%), followed by Vellore (10.8%).
* Patient registrations have grown steadily from 2021 to 2024.
* Gender distribution is approximately 50/50 Male/Female.

### Recommendation:

* Consider opening a satellite clinic or telemedicine service targeting
**Coimbatore and Thanjavur** (lower penetration cities).

\---

## 9\. Revenue Insights

### Finding:

* Treatment revenue (₹46.5L) is \~10x the consultation revenue (₹4.8L),
showing that complex treatments are the primary revenue driver.
* Revenue peaks align with appointment peaks (June–August).

### Recommendation:

* Upsell preventive health packages during high-traffic months.
* Introduce loyalty programs for returning patients to improve LTV.
